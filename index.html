The produce industry is pretty old-fashioned (with some of them even still using fax machines daily), so building a rapport with customers is crucial to the success of the company. I speak with customers daily, guiding them through the designs of new changes they'd like to implement, or working with them to test and debug issues in the system. My main responsibility is still designing, implementing, and maintaining the code, but it involves vastly more consulting work than the average software developer job.

In my time at Produce Pro, I have added countless new features to the system, as well as identified, tested, and resolved hundreds of bugs. I have demonstrated my ability at connecting with the customer and leading them through trips, so I now act as the site-lead on consultation trips. I was also selected to be one of only a few points of contact for Del Monte, our largest customer.

Through my experiences at Produce Pro, I have learned an immense amount about building relationships with customers, fullstack development and seeing a change through the entire development cycle, as well as working in a large but flexible company.

Latest Project

Personal Bar Web Application

Please contact me with name ideas...

One of my somewhat recent hobbies is mixology. As anyone without a "go-to" cocktail knows, choosing a drink can sometimes be a bit of ordeal - even more so without a real menu to look at. My solution to this problem was to build a web application that would have all of my on-hand ingredients stored and provide a list of available cocktails to my guests. When they choose a drink and order it, it provides me with a notification of who ordered what, along with the recipe of the drink they ordered. This saves time in the guest choosing a drink to enjoy, and encourages me to try out drinks I've never made!

I am using a cloud-hosted NoSQL database to store requests, recipes, etc. and am building the web application simply with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for now. Once I get more core functionality I will probably add some more flair. This is still a work in progress, but stay tuned for updates!

University Involvement

One Army

One Army is a service based men’s organization at Texas A&M University. Our foundational pillars are service, leadership, and brotherhood, which embody our purpose. We continually strive to improve as individuals and as an organization by promoting campus unity. The One Army mission is to develop its members as Aggies and leaders by serving the campus and community and promoting unity in Aggieland.

Our main philanthropy is Still Creek Ranch, a non-profit organization which shelters abused and neglected children from around the country. Every Friday, the organization visits the boys at Still Creek Ranch to serve as mentors and support their development in any way possible. In addition, because Still Creek Ranch is a non-profit organization that operates solely off of donations, we organize and facilitate an annual 5k mud run called Gladiator Dash to raise money for them. Though we primarily focus on serving Still Creek Ranch, we serve the community in countless other ways, like volunteering at Twin City Missions, visiting the children at Miracle Farm, participating in Big Event, and supporting other organization's philanthropic endeavors.

Gladiator Dash

Gladiator Dash is a 2.3 mile, obstacle-integrated mud run that takes place each spring. In just my three previous years as a member in One Army, we have had over 5,000 participants and raised more than $150,000. We then donate 100% of these proceeds to Still Creek Ranch.

In addition to helping build all of the obstacles and working the race itself, I serve on the philanthropy committee. My tasks as a member of this committee range from identifying businesses that might want to sponsor our event and securing those donations, to planning our advertising strategy and organizing the logistics of the event.
